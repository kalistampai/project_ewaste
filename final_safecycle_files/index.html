How it works

Option A

Free doorstep pickup

  1. Tell us what you've got — one call, text, or email. Phones, laptops, PCs, tablets, DVRs, cables, the works. Broken is fine. Broken is expected.
  2. Pick a day — choose a date that works, or say "ASAP" and we'll fit you into this week's route.
  3. Set it out — porch, driveway, or hand-off. We confirm by text when it's collected. Done.
Option B

Drop-off at the workshop

  1. Message us first — we'll share the current drop-off address and hours.
  2. Bring your devices — no appointment needed once you have the details.
  3. That's it — we handle data wiping, refurbishing, and responsible recycling from there.

We accept: phones · laptops · desktop PCs · tablets · flat-screen monitors · DVRs & streaming boxes · game consoles · routers & modems · keyboards, mice & cables · hard drives (wiped or destroyed, guaranteed) — and just about any other electronic. If it plugs in or charges, chances are we'll take it. Not sure? Just ask.

Why it matters

One device can change a kid's whole year

Thousands of Bay Area students still don't have a working computer at home. Homework gets done on a cracked phone screen — or doesn't get done at all. Meanwhile, perfectly fixable laptops sit in drawers three blocks away. Safe Cycle Tech exists to close that gap.

What we do with your device

Every donation is triaged by hand in our workshop. If it can live again, we wipe it clean, repair it, load it with free, kid-friendly software, and pass it on. If it can't, we make sure its materials are recovered responsibly by certified California recyclers — so nothing toxic ends up in our soil or water.

Where devices go

Refurbished devices — computers, phones, tablets, anything that can help a student — are donated to families through local channels serving low-income youth across the Bay Area:

  • Title I schools and after-school programs
  • Community centers serving low-income families
  • Youth and family programs across the Bay Area

Our story

Helping Bay Area kids get connected

Safe Cycle Tech is a small Bay Area workshop with one goal: getting students and underprivileged families connected online. We collect the electronics you no longer need, refurbish what we can, and place working devices with local kids who need them. What can't be saved is recycled responsibly.

The need is bigger than most people realize. Even here in the Bay Area, many students still don't have a working computer at home — and today, homework, school portals, college applications, and job searches all happen online. A family without a device gets left behind. Your old electronics can change that.

Straight answers

Questions people actually ask

Is it really free? What's the catch?

Really free, no catch. Refurbishing donated electronics for local kids is the point of the workshop. We may pursue nonprofit status and California's recycling programs to fund growth — but pickups stay free either way.

What happens to my personal data?

Every storage drive is either wiped to NIST 800-88 standards or physically destroyed before a device goes anywhere. We keep a log, and you're welcome to ask for confirmation for your specific device.

My device is completely dead. Do you still want it?

Yes! Dead devices still have valuable, recoverable parts — and hazardous materials that should never hit a landfill. If we can't revive it, it goes to a certified California electronics recycler.

Who receives the devices?

Refurbished devices — computers, phones, tablets, anything that can help an underprivileged student — are donated through schools, after-school programs, and community organizations serving low-income Bay Area families.

Do you take TVs, printers, or big appliances?

Computer monitors — yes, absolutely, as long as they're the flat-screen kind. What we can't take: old tube-style (CRT) TVs and monitors, big-screen TVs, and large appliances — we're a small operation, so oversized and heavy items are beyond what we can haul. For just about everything else, the rule of thumb is simple: if it plugs in or charges and one person can carry it, we'll almost certainly take it. Not sure? Ask — and if it's something we can't handle, we'll point you to the right county drop-off.
